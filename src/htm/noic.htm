There is no sun. No light but for the luminescent ripple awakened by a wave breaking, the spark made by the shatter of metal or the storms igniting the crests of dunes; but here, nothing natural grows with eyes to see it. Eyes came from elsewhere.

Shielded from the winds above, invisible, Den Azar kneels on the wet fiberglass, bends over the feroplastic membrane, bites at the loose shale and succulent cables; masticates, voraciously, my fabric before sinking into a satiated sleep. The blizzard accumulates in mounds of ash around her ceramic legs and pools in the recesses of her closing golden eyes.

The storm blows itself out, the glassy ocean reflects nothing back. A million sunflowers at the bottom of the Abyss stir.

- | -

Den Azar kicks at the miasma, it liquefies, a metallic arc lights up the night. A sunflower notices and immediately loses interest. He swims through the lye, caustic, immaterial, wormlike toward the surface. Two iridescent florets of gold bloom.

"Hummingbirds?"

A throbbing flutter nears and then leaves, and comes back, is joined by another; the air tickles. Far away, a gale exhales a raspy breath. Den Azar coughs the dust of sleep out of her lungs, he emerges a short distance away from the nucleus of the avian excitement. A searing hot flash cracks the night apart, a hundred silhouettes are cast in gold, immobile, mid flight, before the unseeing returns.

He sits and reopens her eyes expecting nothing. The flock is a swarm, now a murmuration and then a violent whirling of needles in the dark. The Abyss recoils in apprehension as a million eyes turn to face, as if seeing through my hollowlessness, the distant implosion of night. The hummingbirds scatter, Den Azar's ears pop. It would be utterly silent if it wasn't for the sizzling of vitrified sand.

A cold uncertain glow that sways at the end of a short stick illuminates a shallow crater in front of Den Azar. He turns her back to shield her eyes against the dreadful light. Den Azar, caught in an uncontrollable shudder, begs for the snuffing of the offending light. He closes her eyes. A single sharp palatal clap reveals the stranger's shape in all its details. The light-stick is clasped by a toothless mouth at the end of slender organs bursting from the silhouette's torso.

The light vanishes, its soft hum goes quiet and, far away, the Abyss relaxes.
