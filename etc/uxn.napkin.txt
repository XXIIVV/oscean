Memory

	A byte is 8 bits
	A short is two big-endian bytes
	A relative byte is a signed byte offset.
	A relative short is a signed short offset.

	There are 2 stacks
	A stack has 0x100 bytes and a pointer
	A pointer is a byte and wraps
	Ram is 0x10000 bytes and wraps
	Dev is 0x100 bytes and wraps

	Rom is loaded at 0x100
	Evaluation starts at 0x100

Standard Opcode Modes

	A standard opcode has 5 bits, and 3 mode bits
	
	0x80 Stack reads don't consume items
	0x40 Operates on the other stack
	0x20 Values are shorts

Standard Opcodes

	 v is a byte or a short
	v1 is always a byte
	v2 is always a short
	#v is read from memory
	.v is on the other stack
	?v may have side-effects
	addr is rel1, abs2 in 0x20 mode

	01          a INC -- a+1
	02          a POP --
	03        a b NIP -- b
	04        a b SWP -- b a
	05      a b c ROT -- b c a
	06          a DUP -- a a
	07        a b OVR -- a b a
	08        a b EQU -- a=b1
	09        a b NEQ -- a≠b1
	0a        a b GTH -- a>b1
	0b        a b LTH -- a<b1
	0c       addr JMP --
	0d cond1 addr JCN --
	0e       addr JSR -- .pc2
	0f          a STH -- .a
	10       abs1 LDZ -- #abs
	11   val abs1 STZ --
	12       rel1 LDR -- #rel
	13   val rel1 STR --
	14       abs2 LDA -- #abs
	15   val abs2 STA --
	16      ?dev1 DEI -- #dev
	17  val ?dev1 DEO --
	18        a b ADD -- a+b
	19        a b SUB -- a-b
	1a        a b MUL -- a×b
	1b        a b DIV -- a÷b|0
	1c        a b AND -- a&b
	1d        a b ORA -- a|b
	1e        a b EOR -- a^b
	1f      a hl1 SFT -- a>>l<<h

Immediate Opcodes

	Immediate opcodes have no modes
	J opcodes read a signed short distance relative to the post-fetch PC

	00            BRK ->
	20      cond1 JCI --
	40            JMI --
	60            JSI -- .pc2
	80            LIT -- #pc
	a0           LIT2 -- #pc2
	c0           LITr -- .#pc
	e0          LIT2r -- .#pc2
