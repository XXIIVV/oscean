    Latin sin Flexion(LsF) is an auxiliary language created by Giuseppe Peano.

    The idea is to strip Classical Latin down to its simplest possible grammar while keeping its vocabulary recognizable, making a language learnable in hours rather than years.

    This page documents a specific variance of LsF that goes a step further and removes trailing vowels for words than one syllable, and repeated consonants.

    Pronunciation

    LsF favors the old Latin pronunciation, vowels are pronounced as indicated below:

    • a, as in father.
    • e, as in they.
    • i, as in machine.
    • o, as in tone.
    • u, as in rule.
    • y, as French u.
    • j, as y in yes.
    • ae, as i in aisle.
    • oe, as oi in boil.

    Consonants are sounded as in English with the exceptions:

    • c, like k always, as in can, cat.
    • h, silent in th, ph, ch, rh, otherwise like English h.
    • y and j, as i in tin.
    • ae and oe, as a in fate or e in get.

    Syntax

    The order of words in LsF is so nearly similar to the English order of words that one may safely follow that usage without fear of being misunderstood or being too greatly incorrect. All grammatical elements are not necessary, declension, conjugation, etc., are eliminated.

    The subject tends to come first, the predicate last, as in English and other languages. The word most expressive of the thought uppermost in mind will likely come first and the others follow in their natural sequence.

    Nouns

    Any Latin dictionary gives two forms for each noun, the first form is called nominative, the second genitive. The LsF nouns are taken from the Latin genitive form, by changing the genitive ending in accordance with the following table:

    Latin LsF English
    Nominative Genitive -- --
    rosa rosае ros rose
    laurus lauri laur laurel
    casus casus casu case
    series seriei seri series
    pax pacis pac peace

    A few nouns are used in their nominative forms to avoid confusion with other words, like mas(male). There is no grammatical or artificial gender. Natural gender is indicated by different names if these are in international use, like patr, matr, fratr, soror, etc.. If different names do not exist, gender is indicated like can mas(male dog), can femin(female dog).

    There are no cases in LsF. The English genitive is translated by "de", as in ped de homin(the man's foot). A "s" marks the plural, but it is omitted when not necessary: tres fils, or tres fil(three sons).

    Nos hab un ling et du aur.

    We have one tongue and two ears.

    The plural "s" is necessary in cases like the following: patr hab fils(the father has sons or daughters), but the phrase could be changed to avoid the use of the plural: patr hab plur fil.

    Articles

    There is no definite or indefinite article in LsF. It is translated with a pronoun, like "il", "un", etc., when it has the value of a pronoun and its use is necessary:

    da ad me libr        = give me the book
da ad me hoc libr    = give me this book.
da ad me il libr     = give me that book.
da ad nne un libr    = give me a book.
da ad me il meo libr = give me that book of mine.
da ad me un meo libr = give me a book mine.
da ad me meo libr    = give me my book.
Ieon es fort         = lions are strong.
hoc leon es fort     = this lion is strong.
fer es util          = iron is useful.
hoc fer es util      = this iron is useful.

    Adjectives

    The Latin vocabulary gives the nominative of the three genders, and in some instances the genitive. The LsF form is obtained:

    • From the nominative neuter:
      • by leaving it unchanged when it ends by e;
      • by changing it to o when it ends in um;
    • From the genitive form in all other instances:
      • by changing to e the ending is as in the case of nouns. 
    Latin                           LsF             English

celeber celebris celebre        celebr         celebrated
novus   nova     novum          nov            new
audax   audax    audacis        audac          audacious

    For the comparison of adjectives, Plus, magis, minus, mult, ultr, extr, etc., are used.

    brev                              = short.
plus brev quam, magis brev quam   = shorter than.
minus brev quam                   = less short than.
mult brev, ultr brev, extr brev   = very short.
maxim brev                        = shortest.
minim brev                        = least short.
tam brev quam                     = as short as.

    Adverbs from adjectives are obtained by means of: cum ment, in mod. For example, to mean diligently, cum ment diligent, cum diligent ment, in mod diligent, in diligent mod. To mean fraternally, in mod fratern or in mod de fratr. Adjectives may be formed by means of "de", like de fratr(fraternal).

    "ben" may be used to render the prefix "well" either separately or combined: benconsiliat(well-advised), malfamat(ill-famed) or malconception(misconception).

    The prefixing of the negative "ne" or "non" may ordinarily be used to denote the contrary. The preposition "sin" may be used to denote lacking, like the suffix "-less" in English.

    Verbs

    The Latin vocabulary gives the present indicative and the present infinitive. By dropping the ending of the infinitive -re, the LsF form is obtained. There are a few exceptions: dic, duc, es, fac, fer, vol.

    To the present form of the verb add:

    • -r for the infinitive
    • -t for the past participle
    • -nt for the present participle
    present             am    = love.
infinitive          amar  = to love.
past participle     amat  = loved.
present participle  amant = loving.

    The form of the imperative is the same as the one for the present. Sometimes the idea of the past is indicated in some word of the sentence and in such case there is no need to inflect the verb.

    me am  = I love.
te am  = thou lovest.
il am  = he, she loves.
nos am = we love.
vos am = you love.
ils am = they love.
    Her me scrib.

    I wrote yesterday.

    When it is necessary to indicate the past, this can be done by an adverb, as "jam" or "tum", particularly used for this purpose, or by "in praeterit" or by "e" preceding the verb: me jam am, me tum am or me e am(I loved).

    Likewise for the future. The idea of time may be implied in some other word of the sentence like: cras nos leg = we will read tomorrow. If it is necessary to indicate the future, it can be done by the expression "in futur", or by the verbs "vol" and "deb" like in English, or by "i" preceding verb: me vol am, me deb am, me i am(I shall or will love).

    The subjunctive has no special ending, its idea is expressed by the use of conjunctions like si, que, ut, quod.

    The passive form is rendered by the past participle and the verb "es", to be: es amat(is loved). The passive may be done away with, as in any language, by changing the sentence: fil es amat ab matr = the son is loved by the mother to: matr am fil = the mother loves the son. It may also be rendered by "quem" and a relative clause: fil es quem matr am = it is the son whom the mother loves.

    Intransitive verbs may be formed with the help of "fi" = become; transitive with the help of "fac" = make. Fi alb = alb fi = albofi = to become white = to whiten (intransitive). Fac alb = alb fac = albofac = to make white = to whiten (transitive). Verbs, however, may be used as transitive or intransitive, if the meaning in that sentence is unequivocal. Other verbs may be formed by means of "es" = to be; red = to render = to make; da = to give. Es caus de = to cause; es flex = to sag; red triste = to sadden; to make sad; red nul = to annul = to render void; da anim = to encourage = to give courage.

    Non es cane simile ad lupo?

    Is not the dog like the wolf?

    Conversation

    In answering "yes" and "no" the speaker repeats the verb or uses one of the following expressions:

    For answering yes:

    cert     = certainly.
etiam    = even so.
it       = true, so, yes.
it ver   = certainly.

    For saying no:

    minim   = by no means.
non     = not so.
nul mod = by no means. [no way!]

    Example

    LsF es lingu universal que omn person in mod facil scrib et intelig sin usu de special stud. Libr in LsF es difus supr plur region de Europ, Americ, Afric, Austral et Asi ubi cultur ocidental es not.

    LsF adopt omn vocabul que exist simil in Angl, German, Franc, Rus, Latin et Graec. Et adopt omn vocabul angl-latin cum form de them(radic). LsF sicut med de script et oral comunication inter populs de var nationalitat es mult superior ad omn al artificial auxiliar lingu pro sequents nation:

    • 1. Suo vocabular non es format ad arbitr, sed consist de vocabuls hodi in usu in var lingu.
    • 2. Pro ist ration il hab maxim praecision de expresion et es un vivent organism.
    • 3. Il es analytic et liber ab mort ponder de gramatic qual, articul, numer, gener, tempor et mod, concordant, etc.
    • 4. Pro suo practic usu, exig nul special libr. Pauc regulas elementar et un dictionari classic- latin in usu comun in lingus modern es suficient, in cas de necesitat pro interpretation de un publication in LsF.
    • 5. LsF es perfect in philologi et hab maxim de internationalitat.

    Usu de LsF es indicat pro scientific, literari, artistic international comunication, pro comercial corespondenti, pro scop social et de viators, etc.

    Suo usu in omn international congresu et per omn fin politic aut scientific facilit inteligenti et economiz mult tempor. LsF es de aequal aequisition ad omn clas de societat que hab mutu interes de comunic inter se.

    In adition LsF es de magn valor educational nam es de logic et natural formation. LsF non pertin ad un societat special sed ad tot mund. LsF non es fact pro servi interes de un nation, clas de homins, aut individu, sed pro red facil progres de gener human.

    LsF non es dogma; regulas de LsF es simplic opinion de majoritat de sectators. Libertat es necesari condition in scienti et in social vit.
