    Thoughts on Latin Sin Flexion

    The first question anyone has upon looking at the ending vowel reduction(Apocope) is whether this creates nonresolvable root word conflicts and homophones. There is three categories of conflicts and each one has a its own solution.

    • Type disambiguation, a noun and a verb becoming the same word. Example: sicca(to dry)/sicco(dried) both become sic. In most cases, this is resolved by using an auxiliary word like in English: cook[n], to cook[v].
    • Similar meaning, but different words. For example, disseminate and seeding, are both "sem". Those tend to resolve of their own.
    • Actual collisions, these are resolved either by preserving their homophony, or by using an alternative word.
